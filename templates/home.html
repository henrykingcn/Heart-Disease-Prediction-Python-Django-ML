Heart Disease
Here are something you need to know about!


What is Heart Disease?

The term “heart disease” refers to several types of heart conditions. The most common type of heart disease in the United States is coronary artery disease (CAD), which affects the blood flow to the heart. Decreased blood flow can cause a heart attack.

What are the symptoms of heart disease?

Sometimes heart disease may be “silent” and not diagnosed until a person experiences signs or symptoms of a heart attack, heart failure, or an arrhythmia. When these events happen, symptoms may include:
 -Heart attack: Chest pain or discomfort, upper back or neck pain, indigestion, heartburn, nausea or vomiting, extreme fatigue, upper body discomfort, dizziness, and shortness of breath.
 -Arrhythmia: Fluttering feelings in the chest (palpitations).
 -Heart failure: Shortness of breath, fatigue, or swelling of the feet, ankles, legs, or neck veins.

What are the risk factors for heart disease?

High blood pressure, high blood cholesterol, and smoking are key risk factors for heart disease. About half of people in the United States (47%) have at least one of these three risk factors. Several other medical conditions and lifestyle choices can also put people at a higher risk for heart disease, including:

 -Hypertension

-Diabetes

-Chronic Kidney Disease

-Obesity

-Abnormal Blood Lipids

Any strategies to prevent heart disease?

These eight key factors can help you lower your risk of heart attack and stroke if you’ve never had one. They’re part of an overall healthy lifestyle for adults.

-Healthy Diet

-Weight Control

-Physical Activity

-Blood Sugar Control

-Blood Pressure Control

-No Smoking

-Positive Thoughts

-No Alcohol
